# Constitution — Part 2: Customer Profile (ClaimPilot)

**ICP:** Independent practices (1–25 providers) in outpatient medical, dental, behavioral health, physical therapy/specialty, and small RCM/billing firms. Geo: US (payer-specific, HIPAA). 

**Buyer:** practice owner/clinician, practice manager, or billing-company principal.
**Daily users:** billers, practice managers, RCM staff.
**Influencer:** owner-clinician (signs clinical appeal content), external billing partner.

**Jobs To Be Done:**
1. "Tell me why this claim was denied and whether it's worth appealing."
2. "Write the appeal for me with the right citations."
3. "Track all my appeals so none fall through the cracks."
4. "Recover money we're currently writing off."

**Pains:** denials silently erase revenue; staff lack time/expertise; enterprise RCM tools target hospitals; appeals are tedious + payer-specific.

**Gains:** recovered cash, less write-off, less manual work, visibility into denial patterns, prevention insight.

**Anti-personas:** large hospital systems (served by Waystar-class); cash-only/no-insurance practices.

**Willingness to pay:** high — ROI is recovered revenue; reseller leverage via billing companies (one buyer → many practices).

**Consistency check:** drives pricing/reseller (Part 3/11), HIPAA scope (Part 7), grounding (Part 6).
