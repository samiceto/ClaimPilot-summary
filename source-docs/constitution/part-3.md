# Constitution — Part 3: Business Model (ClaimPilot)

**Revenue model:** subscription per provider/location + optional performance fee on recovered claims; billing-company reseller tier.

| Tier | Price | For | Limits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solo | $149/mo | 1–2 providers | core denials + appeals, limited volume |
| Practice | $449/mo | small practice | multi-provider, full appeals + tracking, analytics |
| Group | $1,200/mo | multi-location/specialty group | unlimited providers, API, priority, SSO |

**Add-ons:** performance fee (e.g., % of recovered claim revenue, opt-in); reseller/billing-company tier (volume pricing, multi-practice console); denial-prevention analytics.

**Pricing principles:** anchor to recovered cash ("recovers more than it costs in month one"); annual default; land Solo/Practice, expand via providers + performance fee + reseller.

**Unit economics (target):** blended ACV ~$6,000; gross margin **>82%** (HIPAA infra slightly higher COGS); CAC payback < 7 months (longer cycle, offset by very low churn); NRR > 115%.

**Channels:** billing-company resellers (highest leverage), specialty associations, RCM/practice-management communities, denial-rate ROI content.

**Cost discipline:** BAA-covered managed infra; RAG over giant context; cheap/open models for denial classification + extraction, frontier only for appeal drafting/reasoning; PHI minimization reduces handling cost. AI COGS < 10% revenue.

**Consistency check:** supports affordability (Part 1), ICP WTP (Part 2), metrics (Part 12).
