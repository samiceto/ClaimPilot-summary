# Constitution — Part 11: Growth Strategy (ClaimPilot)

**Wedge:** "Recover the denied claims you're writing off — appeals drafted for you." Win independent practices the enterprise RCM tools ignore; lead with billing-company resellers for leverage.

**Acquisition:**
- **Billing-company reseller channel (primary):** one billing firm → many practices; multi-practice console + volume pricing; highest-leverage, lowest CAC.
- **Specialty associations & communities:** dental, behavioral health, PT, specialty groups.
- **ROI content/SEO:** denial-rate benchmarks, "appeal CARC X", recovery calculators.
- **PM/EHR marketplace integrations (post-MVP):** distribution + sticky data.

**Activation:** import an 835/EOB → see diagnosed denials + recoverable $ + first drafted appeal = wow; measure time-to-first-appeal.

**Conversion:** trial / pilot (recover on a sample batch); ROI anchored to recovered cash; performance-fee option lowers adoption risk.

**Retention/expansion:** ROI → very low churn; expand via providers/locations, performance fee, reseller growth, prevention analytics; payer-rules KB compounds (switching cost ↑).

**Moats:** compounding payer-rules + denial-reason KB per specialty/payer; HIPAA-grade infra as barrier to entry; reseller relationships; ROI-driven retention. **Portfolio's long-term defensibility play.**

**Metrics:** CAC payback < 7 mo; NRR > 115%; reseller-sourced %; time-to-first-appeal; recovery rate.

**Consistency check:** matches ICP (Part 2), pricing/reseller (Part 3), HIPAA trust (Part 7), north-star (Part 12).
